# Key Insight

A clock is a circle:

```text id="ac4"
360 degrees
```

---

## Minute Hand

Moves:

```text id="ac5"
360 degrees in 60 minutes
```

So:

```text id="ac6"
360 / 60 = 6 degrees per minute
```

Formula:

```cpp id="ac7"
minute_angle = minutes * 6;
```

---

## Hour Hand

Moves:

```text id="ac8"
360 degrees in 12 hours
```

So:

```text id="ac9"
360 / 12 = 30 degrees per hour
```

Formula:

```cpp id="ac10"
hour_angle = hour * 30;
```

---

## Common Mistake

Many people stop here.

But the hour hand also moves continuously.

Example:

```text id="ac11"
3:30
```

Hour hand is NOT at:

```text id="ac12"
90°
```

It is halfway toward 4.

---

So:

```text id="ac13"
30 degrees per hour
0.5 degrees per minute
```

because:

```text id="ac14"
30 / 60 = 0.5
```

Final formula:

```cpp id="ac15"
hour_angle = hour * 30 + minutes * 0.5;
```

---

# Your Solution

```cpp id="ac16"
double hour_ang =
    (hour * 30) % 360
    + (minutes * 0.5);

double min_ang =
    (minutes * 6) % 360;
```

Correct.

---

## Find Difference

```cpp id="ac17"
double ans = abs(hour_ang - min_ang);
```

---

## Smaller Angle

A circle has two paths:

Example:

```text id="ac18"
20°
```

and

```text id="ac19"
340°
```

We want:

```text id="ac20"
smaller angle
```

So:

```cpp id="ac21"
if (ans > 180)
    ans = 360 - ans;
```

Perfect.
